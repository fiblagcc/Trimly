# Product

## Register

product

(The marketing landing page at `/` is the one brand surface and is designed in the brand
register. Every authenticated screen is product.)

## Users

Two audiences, one product:

- **Clients** looking for a haircut. They land, type a ZIP, and want to see who is actually
  open near them and book a time in a few taps. Mostly on a phone, often in a hurry.
- **Barbers** running an independent chair. They set up a shop, open availability, flip
  their subscription on, and watch bookings arrive. They want to fill the chair without
  playing phone tag.
- **Admins** keep the platform healthy: triage support tickets, read a quick health report.

## Product Purpose

Trimly connects clients with independent barbers. Barbers pay to be listed; clients search
by ZIP, see active shops, and book an open slot. The barber is notified in real time.
Success is a booking completed end to end without a phone call, and a barber whose open
times turn into appointments.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, and direct. Reads like a sharp local-services brand with a magazine's
sense of type and space, not a generic SaaS dashboard. Voice is plain and specific: say
what the product does, skip the hype. Three words: warm, editorial, grounded.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: centered everything, three identical feature cards, a stats
  band with invented numbers, a gradient hero.
- generic template tells: tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, flat icon+title+text
  card grids, glassmorphism, fake ratings and testimonials.
- Corporate coldness. Trimly is a local-services brand, not enterprise software.

## Design Principles

1. **Type and one hero moment carry each screen.** Restraint everywhere else.
2. **Show real product, not claims.** No invented stats, ratings, or testimonials.
3. **One dark surface anchor per layout** for weight and confidence.
4. **Amber is rare and deliberate**, one accent moment per screen.
5. **Plain, specific copy.** Concrete nouns and verbs over marketing language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text meets 4.5:1 contrast (secondary text included, not light gray for
"elegance"). Every input has a visible label, focus rings are visible, the app is keyboard
navigable, and motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
